Replace the current static Cash Out system with a live dynamic Cash Out engine that recalculates the Cash Out offer every time there is any change in any event contained in the user's bet slip.

The Cash Out value must never remain fixed. It should continuously increase or decrease according to the probability of the ticket winning.

Every fixture update received from the live data provider must trigger a complete recalculation.

General Principle

Cash Out represents the amount the bookmaker is willing to pay the customer before the bet settles.

The calculation should be based on:

Current match score
Match minute
Live statistics
Remaining events on the ticket
Original odds
Current probability of winning
House profit margin
Admin-defined risk percentage

The user should always receive an offer that protects the sportsbook while remaining attractive enough to encourage early settlement.

Overall ticket probability should be recalculated by multiplying all current probabilities together, not by averaging them. This naturally reflects how accumulators become less likely as more uncertain selections remain.

The Cash Out amount is then based on the updated overall probability, not the original odds alone.

Suggested Cash Out Formula

A practical model is:

Potential Return = Stake × Total Odds

Current Ticket Probability = Product of all live selection probabilities

Expected Value = Potential Return × Current Ticket Probability

Cash Out Offer = Expected Value × House Factor

User Interface Behaviour

The Cash Out value should update automatically without requiring a page refresh.

When the value changes:

Animate the amount smoothly.
Flash green if the offer increases.
Flash red if the offer decreases.
Show "Cash Out Suspended" when unavailable, with a brief reason if applicable.
Disable the Cash Out button while a recalculation is in progress to avoid accepting stale offers.
Performance Requirements
Recalculate only tickets affected by the latest live event, rather than every open ticket.
Use event-driven updates from the live data feed instead of fixed polling where possible.
Cache live match state to reduce repeated calculations.
Ensure cash-out acceptance is atomic: when the user clicks Cash Out, lock the offer, recalculate one final time using the latest data, and either honor the confirmed amount or present an updated offer if the value changed before acceptance.

This approach produces a dynamic, risk-aware Cash Out system where offers rise as a ticket becomes more likely to win, fall as outcomes become less favorable, and are suspended when the remaining probability is too low or the market is temporarily unstable. It closely mirrors the behavior users expect from leading sportsbooks while allowing the sportsbook to control exposure through configurable risk parameters.
